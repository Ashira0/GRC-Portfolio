# Methodology Notes and Framework References

## Risk Assessment Methodology

Risk scoring in this ISMS follows a 5x5 likelihood-impact matrix aligned with:

- **NIST SP 800-30 Rev 1** -- Guide for Conducting Risk Assessments  
  Available at: https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/Legacy/SP/nistspecialpublication800-30r1.pdf

- **ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 6.1.2** -- Information security risk assessment process

Likelihood and impact are scored independently on a 1-5 scale. Risk Score = Likelihood x Impact. 
Scores are mapped to Low (1-6), Medium (7-12), High (13-19), and Critical (20-25) levels.

## Control Framework

The Statement of Applicability (NB-ISMS-005) assesses all 93 controls across the four themes 
of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A:

- Theme 5: Organisational Controls (37 controls)
- Theme 6: People Controls (8 controls)
- Theme 7: Physical Controls (14 controls)
- Theme 8: Technological Controls (34 controls)

## Canadian Regulatory Context

This ISMS is designed for a federal IT contractor operating in Canada. 
The following Canadian-specific frameworks informed scope, risk identification, and control selection:

**ITSG-33 -- IT Security Risk Management: A Lifecycle Approach**  
Published by the Canadian Centre for Cyber Security (CCCS)  
https://www.cyber.gc.ca/en/guidance/it-security-risk-management-lifecycle-approach-itsg-33

**OSFI B-13 -- Technology and Cyber Risk Management**  
Published by the Office of the Superintendent of Financial Institutions  
https://www.osfi-bsif.gc.ca/en/guidance/guidance-library/technology-cyber-risk-management

**PIPEDA -- Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act**  
Governs the handling of employee and client personal information  
https://www.priv.gc.ca/en/privacy-topics/privacy-laws-in-canada/the-personal-information-protection-and-electronic-documents-act-pipeda/
